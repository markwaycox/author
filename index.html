Hi, meet Mark

I’m the author of three promising novels. Excitingly, a fourth manuscript, a Memoir, is baking in the oven. I also teach children ESL. When I’m not creating interesting material in my hometown, Philadelphia, I’m often traveling the globe absorbing the culture in some exotic place. At this point, I am about having FUN in life!

Well-being: a vital phrase that offers the best-case-scenario for health & harmony on this planet. My blog, LIT UP LIT , lends a platform for the examination of health & wellness topics through creative, at times explosive, literature and other art forms with the intention of providing key insights into today’s pressing issues.

CREATING NEW CULTURE


A Memoir is in the works:


Almost Never Too Late – The Memoir

This book belies the publishing industry’s demand of new, aspiring authors in appealing to a specific group of readers. This is a Global memoir. It is meant to break the traditional chains of grouping people, to be all-inclusive – no matter what genres you prefer, no matter what demographic you are cast into. I am writing this for all citizens of the world, as we are desperately in need of rediscovering common ground. So, to the young up to the old, the poor and the working class folks that make things go up to the richest, most privileged folks. And with long overdue clarification in refuting the disturbingly casual usage of those familiar yet baseless descriptions of people as black-and-white, instead admitting unequivocally the value of the sum of all the cultures of the world that make humanity great: this book is written for you! This memoir’s particular approach is vital because the Global citizen’s common interests are being stolen away by special interests – and at an increasingly rapid rate. My motivation in writing such a memoir is in bringing (back-to-light) those common interests, documenting them through my own telling experiences in order to explain how and why the time has come for this spectacular, historically unprecedented reunification. For it has come perilously clear that, on a global scale, if that does not happen immediately, I’m afraid the first two words of this book’s title will be scraped away forever. Since my youth, it has felt as if my life existed in the shadows of society more so than on its main stage. From being born biracial in the early sixties, to having more empathy in my character than seemed normal. To being molested in a parking lot at nine years old without any consoling or follow-up counseling, to reaching young adulthood only to realize that my birth nation disingenuously offered me an education. These were matters of building trust. In that department, I was left severely lacking. Like so many who face adversity early on I adapted over time. I found a way to face my problems and stand up for myself when it was vital to do so. It was only then that I could let go of those negative experiences that held me down. The process created neutral ground, which then allowed me to create new culture in my life. In those shadows, I began building my own surrogate system of trust, complete with an adjusted value system I felt comfortable with. My self-confidence rose. Solid friendships resulted, and I was on my way. And how sweet it was! Nothing can diminish the growth and accomplishments that occurred over the following decades. San Cristobal de las casas. November 2021. Officially known as Los Estados Unidos Mexicanos, Mexico wasn’t a place I knew that well. Honestly, Mexico wasn’t a place I respected much. Growing up in the United States, most of the things I’d heard about the country were negative (More about that later.) Yet smack in the midst of one of the most tumultuous periods in modern history, I find myself nestled below a historic church on a mossy cobblestoned hill while sitting in a classic bohemian café – mesmerized…
