My Bio

When I was a student at the University of Pittsburg many years ago one of the books my creative writing professor required was by Studs Terkel. Working investigated the meaning of work for a variety of people under different life circumstances. It showed the double-edged significance that work can have on people; from a thriving creativity to toiling 40-plus hours in order to pick up one's paycheck. This quote from the non-fiction novel stuck in my mind:

Picasso can point to a paintingâ€¦A writer can point to a book. Everybody should have something to point to.

The book stirred my curiosity about the quality of work, as opposed to simply weighing the salary component of it. Since that time, I've gained experience as a cabinet maker's helper, union carpenter, fashion model, health store clerk, personal trainer, Thai massage therapist, HE/PE teacher, ESL teacher, caretaker, and now author.

Combined with extensive travel in over 30 countries, these work experiences have further stirred my curious nature. This epiphany has led me to settling my focus on the concept of Wellbeing.

While understanding Wellbeing is a huge challenge, I see it as a concept that can unify many of life's problematic elements. Through it, I aim to know just how we are faring on the human timeline.

What makes us laugh? What make us cry? What genuinely fulfills us, leaving us content and happy? These questions easily culminate into a comprehensive one: Are we a healthy species at this point in history, or a species in decline?
